# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Three overlapping audiences, all important:
1. **Curious intellectuals** — Adults drawn to deep-dive cultural/science content, seeking mystery and wonder. The core TTBOOK audience who value substance over polish.
2. **Podcast discovery** — General listeners finding the show via search/social. Need to immediately understand the value proposition and feel the show's distinctive personality.
3. **Existing community** — Loyal TTBOOK listeners who already know the brand. They want deeper engagement, membership, and to support the mission.

### Brand Personality
- **Voice**: Intellectually curious, warmly mysterious, cosmically playful
- **Tone**: Like discovering a forgotten room in a museum — wonder, intimacy, depth
- **3-word personality**: Curious. Cosmic. Intimate.
- **Emotional goal**: Visitors should feel they've stumbled into something special — not a generic podcast site, but a *cabinet of curiosities* that rewards exploration

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Dark, cosmic, warm — black backgrounds with cream text, green accents, galaxy imagery
- **Theme**: Dark mode (appropriate for the cosmic/nighttime/mysterious brand feel)
- **Typography**: Jost (Futura alternative) for headlines, EB Garamond (Garamond alternative) for body — loaded via Google Fonts
- **Key brand elements**: Cabinet silhouette, cosmic spiral, bracket corners, wave dividers, green (#10A544) as signature accent
- **References**: The brand was designed by Art & Sons — vintage curiosity cabinet meets cosmic exploration
- **Anti-references**: Generic podcast directories, bright/corporate podcast sites, anything that feels like a SaaS landing page

### Design Principles
1. **Cabinet-first**: Every design element should reinforce the "cabinet of curiosities" metaphor — drawers, compartments, discovery
2. **Cosmic restraint**: The galaxy/cosmic elements are powerful but must be used sparingly. When overused, they become wallpaper.
3. **Brand-strict**: Colors (green/black/cream), fonts (Jost/EB Garamond), and visual vocabulary are locked. Improvements come through better spacing, hierarchy, and component design — not new visual elements.
4. **Multi-show ready**: Architecture must accommodate Luminous (a second podcast with its own accent color and visual identity) without breaking the core Wonder Cabinet identity. See [`docs/multi-brand-design-system.md`](./docs/multi-brand-design-system.md) for the operational contract.
5. **Patronage, not paywall**: The paid tier is mission-support, not content-gating. Upgrade surfaces should feel like an invitation, not a gate.

### Future Requirements
- **Luminous podcast**: Second show sharing the same Ghost instance. Has its own accent color (violet `#9A59FF`) and logo treatment but shares layout DNA, typography, and cream background with Wonder Cabinet. Brand finalized May 2026 (Art & Sons) — see [`docs/luminous/brand-guide.md`](./docs/luminous/brand-guide.md) for the canonical reference and [`docs/multi-brand-design-system.md`](./docs/multi-brand-design-system.md) for how the two brands coexist in this theme.
- **Paid membership tier**: Patronage model — supporters get recognition but content stays free. Need tasteful upgrade surfaces that feel inviting, not pushy.
- **Homepage highlight zones**: Three configurable zones in the episode feed to promote non-WC content (newsletter, special series, Luminous episodes) using a tag-based content query system.
